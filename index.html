About

My name is Tayte Waterman, and I’ve always enjoyed exploring how things work—whether that means tinkering with electronics, experimenting with code, or diving into math and physics out of curiosity. I like building small software and hardware projects that connect the digital and physical worlds, and allow learning through doing. Outside of that, I enjoy cooking, reading sci-fi, gaming, traveling, and most of all spending time with my family—especially my energetic three-year-old son, who keeps life fun and full of discovery and always keeps me on my toes.

Professionally, I bring over a decade of experience in the automotive industry, spanning ECU and vehicle-controls development, embedded systems, and software architecture. I currently work as a Software Architect at Daimler Truck AG, helping define the next-generation Software Oriented Architecture for commercial vehicles. I’m passionate about mentoring peers, championing best practices, and bridging software and mechanical systems to create efficient, innovative, and impactful solutions.

I value continual learning and personal growth, and that mindset has led me to pursue a Master’s in Computer Science at Portland State University. Through this program, I’m deepening my understanding of modern software systems, architecture, and the evolving technologies shaping the future of mobility and intelligent systems.

8-Channel MIDI Stepper-Motor Player
Dec 2024
LINK

A joint Raspberry Pi and Arduino based system that reads MIDI files and plays them via stepper motors using the motor frequency to play individual notes. The Raspberry Pi consumes and translates MIDI files into a proprietary set of commands via USB to an Arduino controller responsible for driving the stepper motors themselves. Several example MIDIs and hardware setup details are included.
